John

adamantly refused to witness Alice taking her medication, unable to come to terms with the harsh reality of her Alzheimer's diagnosis. Simultaneously, he started pushing Alice away, rejecting her assistance in finding his glasses.

The disease had begun to affect Alice's behavior, causing her to engage in actions that were uncharacteristic of her former self, such as choosing coffee instead of her preferred tea with lemon.On the other hand, Alice's declining cognitive abilities became more apparent , which made her difficult to differentiate between the voices of Anna and Lydia on the phone.

In a surprising turn of events, Alice embarked on activities reminiscent of her childhood, such as visiting the church—a ritual long forgotten in her adult life. These changes in behavior worried John, as they were stark departures from Alice's usual routines.
