November 2003

As

time went on, Alice's memory deterioration persisted, leading her to forget crucial details like her class syllabus and an upcoming conference in Chicago. Concerned about these symptoms, Alice sought medical advice from Dr. Moyer, who suggested that her forgetfulness could be attributed to either menopause or a potential brain tumor. Anxious to find answers, Alice underwent an MRI scan, but to her relief, the results came back clear at the end of the month.

However, determined to get to the root of her memory issues, Alice made the proactive decision to schedule an appointment with a neurologist. Despite the initial relief of a negative MRI, she recognized the importance of further investigation into her condition and sought specialized medical expertise to uncover the underlying cause of her declining memory.
